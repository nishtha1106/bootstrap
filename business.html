10 more die, 34 trains hit as floods worsen in Assam

GUWAHATI: The death toll in this second wave of flood climbed to 15 with 10 more people reported dead on Sunday as the government called out Army to rescue marooned people and put IAF on standby to assist the administration deal with the worst flood fury in recent years. Rail connectivity to the region has been snapped with 34 trains being hit, while road connectivity has been disrupted across various locations in flood-hit areas.

In the last 24 hours, more than 10 lakh have been affected, taking the total to over 23 lakh after the flood spread over 21 districts from 19 since Saturday. More than 1.83 lakh people have been displaced and have taken shelter in 439 camps set up by the government. Six people have been reported dead in Kokrajhar, three in Bongaigan and one in Biswanath district.
